Relaxation Techniques

Hover on each picture to learn more!

Yoga is a great way to relieve muscle tension, increase serotonin (AKA "happy" chemical), and improve overall sleep and well-being. It is also a great form of mindfulness as it helps you to be in the moment and ground yourself. On a more biochemical level, yoga helps to decrease levels of stress hormones while increasing endorphins and GABA (gamma-aminobutyric acid) which can allievate anxiety and stress.
Art is a great way to relieve stress as it provides a focus and a creative outlet that helps with emotional release and also improves thought processes. Click the image to learn more! Whether you are a beginner or more advanced, doing any type of art can provide a welcome relief and enjoyment from outside stressors. Painting, drawing, sculpting, and more are great paths to try!
Box breathing is a part of deep breathing techniques. These techniques help to slow your breathing, calm your nervous system, and distract your mind. By controlling your nervous system, your heart rate and blood pressure also decreases. As the image shows, these are the steps to box breathing.1) Breathe out slowly, releasing all the air from your lungs. 2) Breathe in through your nose as you slowly count to four in your head. 3) Hold your breath for a count of four. 4) Exhale for another count of four. 5) Hold your breath again for a count of four.Repeat for three to four rounds. You can repeat this for 3-4 rounds.
Walking is a scientifically proven way to reduce stress as it improves blood circulation and flow to the brain and body. Walking in nature can improve self-esteem, mood, and relax your mind. The combination of physical activity and being outdoors provides an amazing way to take a well-needed break from your stressors.

Using relaxation techniques is a great way to relieve stress, anxiety, and more, whether you are a student struggling with a complex assignment or facing problems in your personal life. Many people use a combination of different techniques to relieve tension, but trying out new methods can always lead to finding out a new way to help you be as healthy as possible.
